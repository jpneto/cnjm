The Portuguese Tournament of Mathematical Games

During the day of November, 26, 2004, almost 500 students (aging 7 to 17) from all Portugal join at Lisbon to play six different abstract games.

The Portuguese Tournament of Mathematical Games (CNJM in Portuguese) started months before in more than 200 schools, scattered thru all Portugal, with local tournaments to find what students were the best players.

The games were:

  • Dots'n'Boxes - the CGT game studied by Berlekamp
  • Polyhedrons [1,2,3,] - a puzzle to build three polyhedrons as fast as possible
  • Wari - a Mancala variant
  • Amazons - two armies of eight queens trying to stalemate each other.
  • Pawns - a game to promote one of your eight chess pawns
  • Hex - the famous connection game.

The games were divided by students' age:

  • First cycle (7-10 years) students played Dots'n'boxes, Polyhedrons or Wari
  • Second Cycle (10-12 years) students played Polyhedrons, Wari or Pawns
  • Third Cycle (12-15 years) students played Wari, Amazons or Pawns
  • Secondary (15-17 years) students played Amazons, Pawns or Hex

This meant 12 independent tournaments (a tournament per age per game). The finals happened at the "Knowledge Pavilion" situated at Expo'98 old site. More information can be found at ludicum.org (in Portuguese).
